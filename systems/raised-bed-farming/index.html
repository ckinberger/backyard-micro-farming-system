Raised Bed Farming

Raised bed farming is a controlled soil system that improves drainage, increases root aeration, and allows precise nutrient management compared to in-ground gardening.

How the System Works

This system modifies three key variables:

  • Soil structure: looser, more aerated composition
  • Water control: improved drainage and retention balance
  • Root space: deeper and less compacted growth zone

When optimized, raised beds significantly reduce issues like overwatering and poor drainage.

Best Crops for This System

  • Tomatoes
  • Peppers
  • Lettuce

Optimization Strategies

Performance improves when combined with:

  • Soil Nutrient Balancing
  • Max Yield per Square Foot
  • Water Efficiency Systems

System Summary

Raised beds are most effective when soil, water, and spacing are actively controlled rather than left to natural ground conditions.
