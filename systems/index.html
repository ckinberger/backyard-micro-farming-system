Backyard Farming Systems

Farming systems define how soil, water, light, and plant spacing interact. Choosing the right system directly impacts yield, plant health, and efficiency in small spaces.

Core Systems

  • Raised Bed Farming
    Improves soil control, drainage, and root development for consistent yields.
  • Container Gardening
    Allows flexible placement and precise water and soil management in limited space.
  • Soil Health Ecosystem
    Focuses on nutrient cycling, microbial activity, and long-term soil productivity.
  • Companion Planting
    Uses plant relationships to improve growth, deter pests, and optimize space.

How Systems Affect Results

Each system controls key variables:

  • Soil: nutrient availability and root health
  • Water: retention, drainage, and consistency
  • Light: exposure and plant energy production
  • Spacing: airflow, competition, and growth potential

Poor system choice leads to problems like low yield, yellowing leaves, and overwatering issues.

Optimization Path

After selecting a system, improve performance using:

  • Max Yield per Square Foot
  • Soil Nutrient Balancing
  • Water Efficiency Systems

Then match systems with crops like tomatoes or lettuce for best results.
