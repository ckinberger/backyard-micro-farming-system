Farming Systems Overview

Farming systems are the structural layer of backyard micro-farming. They define how soil, space, water, and plant interactions are organized to produce predictable growth outcomes.

Core Systems

  • Raised Bed Farming
    Controlled soil elevation system that improves drainage, spacing, and nutrient management.
  • Container Gardening
    Portable micro-environments for precision control of soil and water variables.
  • Soil Health Ecosystem
    Biological system driven by microbes, organic matter, and nutrient cycling.
  • Companion Planting
    Spatial optimization system that reduces pests and improves yield interactions.

How Systems Interact

Each system is not isolated. They interact through three shared variables:

  • Water flow dynamics (retention vs drainage balance)
  • Soil nutrient cycling (organic + mineral balance)
  • Spatial competition (root + canopy interaction)

System-Level Insight

Most gardening failures are not crop problems—they are system design failures. A weak system amplifies problems across all crops, while a strong system reduces maintenance and increases yield stability.
